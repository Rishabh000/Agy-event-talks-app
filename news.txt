World News Headlines - June 15, 2026

1. Geopolitics & Conflict
- US-Iran Peace Deal: President Donald Trump announced a landmark peace agreement to end the war between the US and Iran. The US will lift its naval blockade of Iranian ports in the Strait of Hormuz to ease global economic pressures and resume oil shipments. Some details on strait management and nuclear terms remain subject to diverging interpretations.
- G7 Summit: The 52nd G7 summit began in Évian-les-Bains, France. Protests were reported in Geneva as international leaders gathered to discuss global issues and Middle Eastern developments.
- Middle East Tensions: Despite the broader US-Iran agreement, conflicts persist in Lebanon where Israeli operations against Hezbollah continue, though Hezbollah has indicated willingness to adhere to a ceasefire.

2. United States National News
- Agricultural Modernization: The USDA and Department of Education announced a $125 million investment to modernize agricultural research facilities at land-grant universities.
- Visa Fee Ruling: A federal judge struck down a $100,000 H-1B visa fee proposed by the Trump administration, declaring it an unconstitutional tax.
- Base Accident: A B-52 Stratofortress crashed shortly after takeoff at Edwards Air Force Base in California, resulting in eight reported fatalities.

3. World, Sports, & Society

- Social Media Ban: British Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced plans to ban social media for children under the age of 16.
- Term Limits in Hungary: Hungary's parliament passed a constitutional amendment limiting the prime minister's term to a maximum of eight years.
- UK-Japan Deal: The UK and Japan finalized an investment agreement aimed at creating thousands of jobs in Britain.
- Malaysia Scams & Transit: Malaysia is addressing a rise in online scams and preparing for the launch of the LRT3 Shah Alam transit line.
